About the Project

BeTwin-AI

A cutting-edge Digital Twin platform that mirrors real aircraft engines in the virtual world — continuously learning, predicting failures, and extending operational life before anything breaks.

Concept

What is a Digital Twin?

A Digital Twin is a virtual replica of a physical system that is continuously updated with real-world sensor data. For aircraft engines, this means every temperature reading, pressure fluctuation, and vibration pattern is mirrored digitally — enabling AI models to anticipate degradation long before it becomes critical.

BeTwin-AI applies this philosophy to turbofan engine health, leveraging deep learning on NASA's C-MAPSS dataset to predict Remaining Useful Life (RUL) with high accuracy, giving maintenance teams a clear, data-driven operational window.

Methodology

How It Works

01

Data Ingestion

Multi-variate sensor streams from 21 engine sensors are ingested in real-time — temperature, pressure, speed, and more.

02

Preprocessing

Signals are normalised, noisy sensors removed, and temporal windows constructed to capture engine degradation trends.

03

LSTM Prediction

A deep LSTM network learns long-range temporal dependencies and outputs a precise Remaining Useful Life estimate per engine.

04

Alert & Action

Maintenance teams receive actionable RUL forecasts via the dashboard, enabling proactive scheduling before engine failure.
